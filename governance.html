Innovation Information Initiative (i3) Governance Document

Version 1.0 | Effective Date: [Insert Date]

1. Mission and Purpose

The Innovation Information Initiative (i3) is a global community of economists and innovation scholars dedicated to building open, interoperable datasets to advance research on science, technology, innovation, and entrepreneurship. i3 develops high-quality public datasets, shares methodological best practices, and coordinates research infrastructure to democratize access to innovation research data.

2. Organizational Structure

2.1 Steering Committee

The Steering Committee provides strategic direction, oversees operations, and makes major decisions regarding i3’s activities and resources.

Composition: - 5-7 members representing diverse institutions and expertise areas - Members serve staggered 3-year terms (renewable once) - At least one member should represent early-career researchers

Responsibilities: - Set strategic priorities and research agenda - Approve budget and resource allocation - Oversee grant distribution to students and researchers - Approve new major datasets or infrastructure projects - Ensure adherence to open science principles

Decision-Making: - Decisions require a simple majority vote - Quorum: At least 50% of committee members must be present - Meetings held quarterly (minimum)

2.2 Project Lead(s) / Coordinator(s)

Role: Day-to-day operations, community coordination, and implementation of Steering Committee decisions

Responsibilities: - Manage BigQuery repository and technical infrastructure - Coordinate community meetings and communications - Facilitate collaboration among members - Prepare reports for Steering Committee and funders - Serve as primary contact with NumFOCUS

Appointment: Selected by Steering Committee for 2-year renewable terms

2.3 Working Groups

Topic-specific working groups may be established by the Steering Committee or community members to focus on particular datasets, methodologies, or infrastructure needs.

Structure: - Self-organized with designated coordinators - Report progress to Steering Committee quarterly - Open to all community members

3. Membership

3.1 Community Members

Eligibility: Researchers, students, and practitioners actively engaged in innovation research or dataset development

Rights: - Participate in working groups - Attend community meetings - Contribute to datasets and tools - Propose new projects or initiatives - Vote in community surveys (non-binding)

Process: Self-identification via community registration or participation

3.2 Steering Committee Selection

Nomination Process: - Annual call for nominations (self-nominations accepted) - Nominees must be active community members - Current Steering Committee reviews nominations

Selection: - Current Steering Committee votes on new members - Considerations: expertise diversity, institutional diversity, geographic representation, career stage

4. Financial Management

4.1 Budget Authority

The Steering Committee approves annual budgets and major expenditures (>$5,000).

4.2 Funding Sources

  • Donations from individuals and institutions
  • Grant funding from foundations
  • Institutional support from partner organizations

4.3 Expenditure Categories

  • Data infrastructure maintenance
  • Student and researcher grants for dataset development
  • Community meetings and workshops
  • Documentation and outreach

4.4 Grant Allocation

Process: - Open calls for proposals issued at least annually - Review by Steering Committee or designated review panel - Awards based on: alignment with i3 mission, feasibility, potential impact, open science commitment

Requirements: - All funded work must produce openly licensed datasets - Recipients must document methods and provide metadata - Regular progress reports required

4.5 Financial Reporting

  • Quarterly financial reports to Steering Committee
  • Annual financial summary shared with community
  • Full transparency of fund usage via NumFOCUS reporting

5. Decision-Making Processes

5.1 Major Decisions

Require Steering Committee approval: - Strategic direction changes - Budget allocation - Major partnerships or collaborations - Changes to governance structure - Expenditures >$5,000

5.2 Operational Decisions

Project Lead(s) have authority over: - Day-to-day operations - Infrastructure maintenance - Community coordination - Expenditures <$5,000

5.3 Community Input

The Steering Committee seeks community input on major decisions through: - Community surveys - Open meetings - Working group feedback - Annual community meetings

6. Code of Conduct

6.1 Principles

i3 is committed to fostering an inclusive, respectful, and collaborative community. All members must: - Treat others with respect and professionalism - Value diverse perspectives and contributions - Adhere to principles of open science and reproducibility - Properly attribute and cite others’ work - Protect confidential or sensitive information

6.2 Violations

Reports of Code of Conduct violations should be submitted to [designated contact]. The Steering Committee will investigate and determine appropriate actions, which may include warnings, temporary suspension, or removal from the community.

7. Intellectual Property and Licensing

7.1 Default License

All datasets, code, and documentation produced by i3 or with i3 funds should be released under permissive open licenses: - Data: CC0 or CC-BY 4.0 - Code: MIT, Apache 2.0, or BSD - Documentation: CC-BY 4.0

7.2 Attribution

Contributors retain credit for their work. i3 requires proper attribution and citation of datasets and tools.

7.3 External Contributions

Contributors who use i3 infrastructure or funding agree to license their outputs openly as specified above.

8. Amendments

This governance document may be amended by: - Two-thirds vote of the Steering Committee - Community consultation period (minimum 30 days)

9. Dissolution

In the event of dissolution: - All remaining funds will be transferred another organization supporting open science - All datasets and tools will remain publicly available - Documentation will be archived in long-term repositories
